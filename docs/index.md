Having spent longer than we’d care to admit, this site captures some of our experiences from our Enterprise Architecture practice: making it work, how it has evolved, and unsurprisingly how we feel Enterprise Architecture is required more than ever.

Our drivers for this arise from our shared view that Enterprise Architecture, in whatever form it is being delivered, requires inter-personal skills as much as it requires formal approaches, and yet there is a significant focus on the technocracy of Enterprise Architecture , maybe at a cost of benefits realisation.

Our thoughts and reflections are presented as little vignettes,  roughly separated as:

- **Reflecting** - We came, we saw, we experienced, and we would like to help you not to fall into the same traps as maybe we did
- **Thoughts** - in an area dominated by formal certifications and methods, but latterly a paucity of research finding, we develop heuristics that we think may help explain some of the things we see happening in real-world situations

Given our feelings on the necessity of what Enterprise Architecture should be doing to support organisations, and thus how we develop the goodness that Enterprise Architecture can bring. 

We hope you enjoy reading our thoughts, largely garnered from our conversations, that you forgive our maybe idiosyncratic styles of presentation, and that if you take away any thoughts that you find helpful then our work here has been of value.

We also welcome any conversation about what is posted here, whether in agreement or otherwise. It is through discourse that we develop.
